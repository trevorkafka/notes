# Prisms
The volume of a **prism** can be found by multiplying the base area of the prism $A$ by the height of the prism $h$. The prism itself may be slanted without any change to the volume formula, but the measurement of $h$ must be done perpendicular to the base. A **cylinder** is just a special type of prism with a circle as the base (in this case $A = \pi r^2$).

>[!note] Volume of a prism
>$$V = Ah$$
# Pyramids
The volume of a **pyramid** can be found by computing *one third of* the product of the base area of the prism $A$ by the height of the prism $h$. The pyramid itself may be slanted without any change to the volume formula, but the measurement of $h$ must be done perpendicular to the base.  A **cone** is just a special type of pyramid with a circle as the base (in this case $A = \pi r^2$).

>[!note] Volume of a pyramid
>$$V = \frac{1}{3} Ah$$

Students who have completed their first calculus course should be able to derive this formula by performing an appropriate integral.
# Spheres
The volume of a **sphere** with radius $r$ is given by the formula below.
>[!note] Volume of a sphere
>$$V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$$

Students who have completed their first calculus course should be able to derive this formula by performing an appropriate integral.
