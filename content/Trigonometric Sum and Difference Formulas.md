For any angles $A$ and $B$, the following relationships hold for sine and cosine.

>[!note] Sine and Cosine Sum and Difference formulas
>$$
>\begin{align*}
>\sin(A \pm B) &= \sin A \cos B \pm \cos A \sin B \\
>\cos(A \pm B) &= \cos A \cos B \mp \sin A \sin B 
>\end{align*}
>$$

These results can be derived through purely geometric arguments that will be added to this page at a future point.

The notation $\mp$ indicates that the sign here is taken to be opposite to the sign of $\pm$. For clarity, the formulas in the box above really are a condensed version of the following four formulas. Notice how the cosine signs are "flipped" compared to those of sine.
$$
\begin{cases}
\sin (A + B) = \sin A \cos B + \cos A \sin B \\
\sin (A - B) = \sin A \cos B - \cos A \sin B \\
\cos (A + B) = \cos A \cos B - \sin A \sin B \\
\sin (A - B) = \cos A \cos B + \sin A \sin B
\end{cases}
$$
